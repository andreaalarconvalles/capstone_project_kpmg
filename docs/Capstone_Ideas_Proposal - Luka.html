Section 02 — Our Proposals

Five original project ideas

Each occupies a distinct stakeholder lane, leverages the Airbnb dataset differently, and aligns with a real KPMG service line. All verified as non-overlapping with peers.

01
Host Revenue Intelligence Platform
B2B SaaS Prototype · Property Management
Client: Airbnb Hosts & Property Managers
The Problem

Individual Airbnb hosts and small property management companies are leaving 20–35% of potential revenue on the table. They set prices manually, ignore demand seasonality, write weak listings, and have no idea how their reviews compare to competitors. Tools like AirDNA and Guesty exist but cost $500–2,000/month — beyond reach for most hosts.

Our Solution

An AI-powered revenue optimization system that acts as a personal revenue manager. It combines dynamic pricing recommendations, NLP-driven listing quality scoring, competitive sentiment benchmarking, and a GenAI coach that rewrites your listing description to maximize conversion.

Agent Architecture
01 Pricing Agent XGBoost + SHAP to recommend optimal nightly price. Explains which factors are suppressing your price.
02 Demand Agent Prophet time-series forecasting. Predicts occupancy 30/60/90 days out with confidence intervals.
03 Sentiment Agent NLP (BERT/DistilBERT) on reviews. Benchmarks your property against top 10% performers by neighbourhood.
04 Listing Coach Agent GPT-4o rewrites title, description and amenity list. A/B scores old vs new before recommending.
05 Revenue Simulator Combines pricing + demand to project monthly revenue under current vs. recommended strategy.
06 Orchestrator LangGraph supervisor. Routes host query, aggregates outputs into a single Revenue Action Brief PDF.
Tech Stack
XGBoost + Optuna SHAP explainability Prophet forecasting DistilBERT / VADER NLP GPT-4o listing gen LangGraph orchestration Streamlit UI ReportLab PDF output
Business Outcome & KPMG Angle

Estimated host revenue uplift: 18–30% per property. At scale, KPMG could offer this as a white-label advisory tool to property management firms (PMCs) managing 10–500 units. Aligns with KPMG's HyperAutomation and AI Factory service lines. Real market comparables: AirDNA ($299/mo), Wheelhouse ($20/mo per listing), PriceLabs ($19.99/mo). A professional-grade KPMG-backed version charging PMCs $99–299/month per portfolio represents a clear commercialization path.

Distinct from peers: ARIA targets institutional fund managers deciding where to invest. This targets active hosts already operating — optimizing yield, not making entry decisions. Zero overlap with Regulators (enforcement tool).
02
Gentrification Early Warning System
ESG Intelligence · Housing Policy · Civic Tech
Client: City Governments & ESG-Focused Investors
The Problem

Rising Airbnb density is one of the most reliable early signals of neighbourhood gentrification and displacement — yet no city currently uses STR data proactively for housing policy decisions. By the time rents spike visibly, the damage is already done. Barcelona saw rents rise 68% between 2015 and 2026. Málaga now has more tourist beds than resident beds in some districts. Housing authorities are always 18 months behind the data.

Our Solution

A geospatial AI system that ingests Airbnb density, pricing trends, and review growth as forward-looking signals of gentrification pressure. It forecasts displacement risk by neighbourhood 12–24 months out, and generates policy briefs recommending intervention thresholds (rent caps, STR quotas, rezoning flags) for city planners.

Agent Architecture
01 Trend Agent Detects STR density growth trajectories per neighbourhood using rolling time-series analysis.
02 Displacement Forecast Agent XGBoost classifier predicts neighbourhoods at high displacement risk within 12–24 months.
03 Geo Mapping Agent Folium/GeoPandas choropleth maps. Visualises risk scores overlaid on neighbourhood GeoJSON boundaries.
04 Policy RAG Agent ChromaDB + LangChain over housing policy documents. Retrieves relevant precedents per risk flag.
05 Alert Agent Monitors threshold triggers (e.g. >15% YoY STR growth) and escalates neighbourhoods for review.
06 Policy Brief Agent GPT-4o synthesises findings into an executive briefing for city housing departments.
Tech Stack
XGBoost classifier Prophet time-series GeoPandas + Folium ChromaDB RAG LangChain + GPT-4o LangGraph multi-agent Inside Airbnb GeoJSON
Business Outcome & KPMG Angle

ESG and public sector advisory are fast-growing KPMG revenue lines. Cities like Amsterdam, Lisbon, and Barcelona are actively paying consultants to quantify STR impact on housing. A prototype of this system is a direct pitch to KPMG's ESG practice and their Public Sector advisory arm. Additionally, ESG-focused real estate funds (e.g. impact investors) would pay for early displacement signals to avoid reputational risk in target markets.

Distinct from peers: Regulators' tool is reactive enforcement (catch illegal listings today). This is proactive policy intelligence (predict displacement 12–24 months ahead). Different user, different data use, different output. No overlap with ARIA (investment returns focus).
03
STR Financial Crime Detection System
AML · Forensic Compliance · Financial Crime
Client: KPMG Forensic Practice · Financial Intelligence Units
The Problem

Short-term rental platforms have become a vector for money laundering. Ghost listings (fake properties that never host a guest), circular booking schemes (where cash is "laundered" through fake bookings), and price manipulation are being used by organised crime. A 2023 FinCEN alert flagged STR platforms as an emerging AML risk. No current tool systematically analyses Airbnb data for financial crime patterns — and this is squarely in KPMG's Forensic & Financial Crime practice.

Our Solution

A multi-agent financial crime detection system that analyses Airbnb listing data for anomalous behavioural patterns consistent with money laundering: ghost listings, price outliers that defy market logic, hosts with implausibly high booking volumes at artificial prices, and accounts with suspicious identity clustering.

Agent Architecture
01 Anomaly Detection Agent Isolation Forest + DBSCAN to flag statistical outliers in pricing, availability, and booking patterns.
02 Ghost Listing Agent Cross-references listing age, review count, response rate and availability to detect phantom properties.
03 Network Graph Agent NetworkX graph analysis to detect hosts with suspicious identity clustering or circular booking rings.
04 Risk Scoring Agent XGBoost classifier assigns AML risk score (0–100) per listing with SHAP-explained contributing factors.
05 Regulatory RAG Agent RAG over FATF, FinCEN, and EU AML directives. Cites which regulations are triggered per flag.
06 SAR Draft Agent GPT-4o drafts a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) ready for analyst review and submission.
Tech Stack
Isolation Forest DBSCAN clustering NetworkX graph analysis XGBoost + SHAP ChromaDB + LangChain RAG LangGraph orchestration GPT-4o SAR generation
Business Outcome & KPMG Angle

KPMG's Forensic & Financial Crime practice is one of the firm's fastest-growing service lines, globally billed at $2–5M+ per AML engagement. A working prototype demonstrating AI-assisted SAR generation from STR data is a direct commercial proof-of-concept for financial regulators (FinCEN, EBA, Bank of Spain) and for Airbnb itself, which faces increasing regulatory pressure to implement AML controls. This is the only capstone idea that touches KPMG's forensic practice — making it completely unique.

Distinct from peers: The Regulators tool detects illegal tourist licensing (administrative law). This detects financial crime (AML/criminal law). Completely different legal domain, different data signals (financial behaviour patterns vs. listing compliance), different output (SAR vs. enforcement brief). No overlap with ARIA.
04
Tourism Demand Forecasting for City Infrastructure
Urban Planning · Public Sector · Smart Cities
Client: City Governments · Tourism Ministries · Urban Planners
The Problem

City governments spend billions on infrastructure planning — metro expansions, waste collection routes, emergency services coverage — based on census data that is 5–10 years old. Airbnb data is in fact the most accurate real-time signal of where tourists are sleeping, in what density, and how that's changing week by week. No city currently uses STR data systematically for infrastructure load forecasting — even though it could reduce planning lag by years.

Our Solution

A demand forecasting intelligence system that uses Airbnb calendar and listing data to predict neighbourhood-level tourist population 30/60/90/365 days ahead. It models the downstream impact on public infrastructure — waste, transit, water, emergency services — and outputs scenario-based infrastructure planning briefs for city operational teams.

Agent Architecture
01 Occupancy Forecast Agent Prophet + SARIMA ensemble. Forecasts tourist-nights per neighbourhood at 30/60/90/365-day horizons.
02 Load Impact Agent Translates tourist-nights into infrastructure load estimates (waste kg, transit trips, water litres) using calibrated multipliers.
03 Spatial Agent GeoPandas + Folium. Maps infrastructure stress hotspots over time. Highlights high-risk zones before peak season.
04 Scenario Agent Monte Carlo simulation of high/base/low tourism scenarios. Quantifies cost of under- vs. over-provisioning.
05 Policy RAG Agent Retrieves smart city planning frameworks and EU urban policy regulations relevant to each stress flag.
06 City Brief Agent GPT-4o generates structured operations briefing: district by district, season by season, with budget implications.
Tech Stack
Prophet + SARIMA Monte Carlo simulation GeoPandas + Folium ChromaDB RAG LangGraph agents GPT-4o synthesis Streamlit dashboard
Business Outcome & KPMG Angle

KPMG's Government & Public Sector practice manages multi-year contracts worth €10–50M+ with city and national governments across Europe. This prototype is directly pitchable to Madrid, Barcelona, and Lisbon's city infrastructure teams — cities already struggling with overtourism. A 10% improvement in infrastructure pre-positioning based on accurate forecasting translates to millions in avoided emergency spend. This also aligns with EU Smart City 2030 initiatives, where KPMG is an active advisor.

Distinct from peers: Regulators enforces housing law. This forecasts infrastructure load for operational planning. Completely different problem domain and client. ARIA is investment advisory; this is urban operations advisory. Distinct data use: calendar.csv availability data (occupancy signals) vs. listing compliance data.
05
STR Market Entry Advisor for Property Developers
Real Estate Development · Site Selection · ROI Intelligence
Client: Real Estate Developers · Private Equity RE Funds
The Problem

Real estate developers building new residential or mixed-use projects need to know: which specific neighbourhoods in a city are supply-constrained for short-term rentals relative to actual demand? Getting this wrong means building in an oversaturated market (low yields) or a market with hidden regulatory risk (a ban incoming). Current approaches rely on gut feel, broker opinion, or expensive bespoke consultancy reports that are already 6 months old by the time they arrive.

Our Solution

An AI system that cross-analyses supply/demand imbalances in STR markets, regulatory risk scores, and revenue yield projections to rank neighbourhoods by development opportunity. Given a budget and city preference, it outputs a ranked site selection brief with a go/no-go recommendation backed by evidence, ready for a developer's investment committee.

Agent Architecture
01 Supply-Demand Imbalance Agent Calculates occupancy-to-supply ratio per neighbourhood. Flags where demand exceeds current inventory.
02 Revenue Yield Agent XGBoost price prediction + occupancy forecast → projected annual revenue yield per sq.m. by zone.
03 Regulatory Risk Agent RAG over local STR regulations + trend analysis. Scores each neighbourhood's 3-year regulatory risk (likelihood of restrictions).
04 Competitive Saturation Agent Clustering analysis on existing host profiles. Identifies zones dominated by professional operators vs. accessible to new entrants.
05 Ranking Agent Weighted multi-criteria scoring (yield × supply gap × regulatory safety × competition). Produces ranked neighbourhood shortlist.
06 Site Selection Brief Agent GPT-4o generates investment committee-ready brief: top 3 sites, rationale, risks, projected 5-year yield.
Tech Stack
XGBoost yield prediction K-Means / DBSCAN clustering Multi-criteria weighted scoring ChromaDB regulatory RAG LangGraph orchestration GPT-4o brief generation GeoPandas + Folium maps
Business Outcome & KPMG Angle

Real estate deal advisory is a core KPMG service line — a single residential development project represents €50–500M+ in capital decisions. Getting site selection right or wrong is worth tens of millions to a developer. KPMG's Real Estate advisory practice regularly supports developers on site feasibility; this AI system turns a 6-week manual analysis into a 10-minute AI-generated brief. Directly pitchable to private equity RE funds and large residential developers across Spain, Portugal, and France.

Distinct from ARIA: ARIA advises institutional fund managers on whether to invest in existing STR markets as a fund. This advises property developers on which specific sites to build on. Different user (developer vs. fund manager), different decision (build vs. allocate capital), different ML focus (site scoring vs. portfolio allocation). No overlap with Regulators.

Section 05 — Next Steps

How to move forward

Once an idea is selected, the project follows a structured 4-week delivery arc matching KPMG's expected capstone format.

1
Select one idea (or hybrid) Choose the direction with the strongest team fit and business story. Ideas 03 (AML) and 04 (Infrastructure) are the most unique. Ideas 01 and 05 have the clearest commercial path. Idea 02 has the strongest ESG narrative.
2
Download data & define cities (Week 1) Select two Airbnb cities from Inside Airbnb (insideairbnb.com/get-the-data). Recommended pairs: Paris + Barcelona (Ideas 02, 03, 04) · Paris + Athens (Ideas 01, 05). Download listings.csv, calendar.csv, reviews.csv, neighbourhoods.geojson.
3
Build ML models & tool contracts (Week 2) Train and validate core ML models (XGBoost/Prophet/clustering). Define tool contracts (TypedDict schemas) before building LangGraph agents. Gate: all models independently testable before agent integration.
4
Build agents & Streamlit UI (Week 3) LangGraph supervisor + specialist agents + synthesis agent. Streamlit demo with HITL approval button. Feature freeze end of Week 3 — no new agents or data sources after this point.
5
Evaluate, stress test & build KPMG deck (Week 4) 15 stress-test queries. Document routing accuracy. Finalise ReportLab PDF output. Build 12–15 slide KPMG presentation. Rehearsal session. Live demo ready.
