Section 01 — Problem Statement

The challenge we are solving

The narrative anchor for the entire project. Every model, every agent, every design decision ties back to this problem. It is what we pitch to KPMG and what every team member should be able to articulate in 60 seconds.

KPMG Lighthouse Capstone — Problem Statement

Short-term rental investment decisions are made on intuition.
They should be made on intelligence.

The European STR market is growing at 11.5% CAGR toward $81 billion. Institutional and individual investors are deploying capital into urban STR assets — yet their tools remain a patchwork of static dashboards, manual research, and heuristic judgements. Two fundamental problems exist simultaneously: pricing opacity (no system explains why a listing commands twice the nightly rate of a similar property two kilometres away) and regulatory blindness (a wave of new STR legislation — Paris's 90-night annual cap, Athens's central district freeze — reshapes risk without queryable intelligence to quantify it).

The result: investors overpay for overcrowded markets or miss emerging opportunities — not because the data does not exist, but because there is no system that reasons across it, explains its conclusions, and delivers them in a format a decision-maker can act on.

🔍

Pricing opacity

Listing prices vary 3–5× within the same neighbourhood with no transparent, data-backed explanation. Hosts underprice; investors miscalculate yield. ARIA's XGBoost + SHAP layer solves this.

⚠️

Regulatory blindness

STR regulation is fragmented, city-specific, and changing rapidly. ARIA's RAG compliance layer indexes AMA regulations and Loi Le Meur, citing the specific article per query.

📉

Supply health risk

Host churn and platform quality decay are invisible until they affect occupancy. ARIA's LightGBM classifier flags at-risk hosts before they exit — a leading indicator for investors.

Our answer: ARIA

ARIA — Agentic Real-estate Intelligence Advisor — is a multi-agent AI system built for STR investment intelligence. Five specialist ML agents orchestrated via LangGraph, with a Streamlit demo and ReportLab PDF output. It does not produce a dashboard. It produces a decision.

Research question

Can a multi-agent AI system reason across pricing dynamics, occupancy forecasts, host risk signals, and regulatory constraints to generate an explainable, evidence-based STR investment recommendation for Paris and Athens?

Hypothesis

A LangGraph-orchestrated system combining XGBoost pricing, Prophet demand forecasting, LightGBM host risk classification, RAG regulatory intelligence, and a GPT-4o listing coach will produce more explainable and more comprehensive recommendations than any single-model approach.

◈ Why this maps to KPMG Lighthouse's five portfolios

Advanced Analytics: XGBoost + SHAP pricing model. Generative AI: GPT-4o listing coach + synthesis agent. AI Agents: LangGraph supervisor + 5 specialist nodes. Trusted AI & Governance: Human-in-the-loop approval checkpoint + leakage detection methodology. New Platform Lab: Streamlit interface + ReportLab PDF investor brief.

The contrast is structural, not superficial. Paris requires price optimisation (size-driven, vintage data ceiling, tightening regulation). Athens requires entry-timing decisions (quality+location-driven, explosive growth, regulatory window narrowing). These two problems require different analytical tools, which is exactly why building one system that handles both demonstrates genuine technical breadth. The EDA confirmed the structural divergence: separate models are not just appropriate — they are required.

Spearman ρ = −0.473 (Athens) vs −0.171 (Paris). This is the primary justification for separate city models. A combined model would wash out the Athens distance signal — which is the dominant pricing variable in that market.

IAB occupancy cap at 255 days is a methodology cap, not a data error

Inside Airbnb's confirmed hard cap is 70% of 365 = 255.5 days. 1,444 Athens listings hit this cap. All downstream Prophet and yield calculations account for this ceiling.

137 unlicensed Athens listings — ~€1.03M redistributable revenue

Pricing at market rates (no significant price difference from licensed listings). If regulations force exit, their revenue redistributes to compliant operators. This is the investor acquisition signal for Phase 5 RAG.

The data reshaping step

Prophet requires ds (date) and y (value) format. The mega dataset is listing-level — aggregate to monthly neighbourhood-level occupancy before fitting.

# Filter to Prophet-eligible Athens rows df = mega[(mega['city']=='athens') & (mega['prophet_training_eligible']==1)].copy() # Convert annual occupancy estimate to monthly proxy df['monthly_occ'] = df['estimated_occupancy_l365d'] / 12 # Use last_review as date proxy, group to monthly city aggregate df['ds'] = pd.to_datetime(df['last_review']).dt.to_period('M').dt.to_timestamp() monthly = df.groupby('ds').agg(y=('monthly_occ','mean'), review_growth_24_25=('review_growth_24_25','mean')).reset_index() # Fit Prophet from prophet import Prophet m = Prophet(seasonality_mode='multiplicative') m.add_regressor('review_growth_24_25') m.fit(monthly) future = m.make_future_dataframe(periods=12, freq='M') future['review_growth_24_25'] = monthly['review_growth_24_25'].median() forecast = m.predict(future)

◈ The three questions the KPMG panel will ask — and your answers

"Why XGBoost and not a neural network?" XGBoost with SHAP is correct for tabular data at this scale (63,520 observed-price Paris rows, 14,242 Athens rows). At this data volume, gradient-boosted trees consistently match or outperform neural networks on structured tabular inputs (Grinsztajn et al., 2022). More critically, SHAP interpretability is non-negotiable for an investor-facing system — a neural network answer without an explanation is a liability in a compliance-adjacent context.

"Why are Paris and Athens models separate?" The EDA confirmed structural divergence: Athens distance effect is 2.8× stronger than Paris (Spearman ρ = −0.473 vs −0.171). SHAP confirms this in the trained models — Athens ranks quality+location (review_score_composite and dist_km at ranks 2–3), Paris ranks size (person_capacity and bedrooms). A combined model would produce worse predictions for both cities by averaging away these structurally different signals. The decision is evidence-based, not arbitrary.

"How do you validate the agent's recommendations?" Three layers: ML models are evaluated on held-out test sets with standard metrics in the evaluation appendix. The RAG compliance agent cites the specific retrieved regulation passage — every claim is traceable to a source. The synthesis agent's output requires HITL human approval before the PDF is generated — the system explicitly acknowledges it requires human review before a recommendation is acted on.
